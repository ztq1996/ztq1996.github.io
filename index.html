About Me

I am an observational cosmologist who works with large extragalactic datasets from the Rubin Observatory, the Hyper Suprime-Cam on the Subaru Telescope, and the Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope to study the large-scale structure of the Universe and the nature of dark matter and dark energy. My research employs techniques such as weak gravitational lensing and galaxy clustering, and focuses on observational systematics that can bias these analyses, including the Point Spread Function (PSF) and photometric redshift (photo-z) estimation.

I am a Research Assistant Professor at the University of Pittsburgh and a LINCC Frameworks Research Scientist. I received a Ph.D. in Physics from Carnegie Mellon University. I completed my undergraduate studies at Duke University and Shanghai Jiao Tong University. I have been passionate about astronomy since I was 15.

I develop and adapt Machine Learning techniques for photometric redshift estimation and feature extraction. In 2018, I have contributed to Natural Language Processing at IBM.

I am based in Pittsburgh, PA, and occasionally visit the Bay Area in California.

Outside of research, I enjoy hiking, community-building in astronomy, and playing basketball.

Research

My research studies weak gravitational lensing and utilize statistical and machine learning methods to extract information from large photometric survey dataset such as the Vera R. Observatory's LSST, the Subaru Telescope's Hyper Suprime-Cam (HSC), and Roman Space Telescope's High Latitude Imaging Survey.

Point Spread Function (PSF)

I study how atmospheric turbulence and optical effects—described as the Point Spread Function (PSF)—impact weak lensing measurements, with particular focus on higher moments and their effects on shear estimation.

PSF higher moments pathfinding (2022)
Comprehensive PSF higher moments study (2023)
PSF systematics mitigation techniques (2023)
My PSF higher moments measurement algorithm is implemented in the Rubin Science Pipeline.

Photometric Redshift with RAIL

I co-lead Redshift Assessment Infrastructure Layers (RAIL), an open-source software platform for photometric redshift estimation and research.

Photo-z Estimation for Rubin Data Preview 1 (2025)
RAIL v1.0: Rubin-era Photo-z Stress Testing (2025)
Forecasting Photo-z Impact on LSST 3×2pt (2026)

Image Simulation & Roman Space Telescope

I extensively apply image simulation with GalSim in weak lensing research and contribute to Roman Space Telescope PSF and shear studies.

Chromatic PSF Effects on Roman (2025)
Roman Image Coaddition Analysis (2024)

Weak Lensing Cosmology with HSC Y3

I developed point-mass correction and redshift self-calibration models to improve weak lensing cosmological constraints from HSC Y3.

Data Compression & Statistical Methods

I develop statistical and machine learning methods for cosmological analysis, including data compression for cosmic shear and neural network approaches for image processing.

Student-Led Research Projects

I mentor students on diverse research topics spanning PSF studies, photo-z, shear analysis, and astrometry. Selected mentored projects that resulted in publications include Roman PSF studies, coadded image PSFs, and real-space vs. harmonic-space shear consistency.
